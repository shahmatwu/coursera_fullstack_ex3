Step 1: Complete a Training Program No standard education requirements exist for image consultants, but training programs are available in the field. These programs may cover certain aspects of fashion, such as colors and business wear, as well as areas such as nutrition and communication. Some image consultants may pursue a degree in mass communication or public relations, which provide information on effective interpersonal communication, public speaking and relaying specific messages. Additional courses in marketing and finance can help independent consultants effectively promote their services to clients and operate a budget. Step 2: Gain Work Experience Prospective image consultants may first gain experience working with a public relations or fashion marketing firm to understand how image affects the overall branding of a client. These opportunities can also help consultants to learn about targeted audiences and how companies and people project a certain image. They may also start their careers by working as part-time consultants until they can create a portfolio and build their clientele. Potential clients include businesses looking to improve the quality of their workers' dress style and celebrities who need styling services for an event. Public figures, such as politicians, may also hire image consultants to develop a presentable look before an appearance. Any company that deals with fashion may use image consultants for various projects. Step 3: Join a Professional Organization Groups such as the Association of Image Consultants International offer networking, continuing education and certification opportunities to their members. Image consultants who join these types of organizations may be able to further their careers. To become an image consultant, you'll need to complete a training program and gain experience in the field.
